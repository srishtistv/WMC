ABOUT WMC
(INDIAN NATIONAL COMMITTEE)

Indian National Committee World Mining Congress

The World Mining Congress (WMC) was initiated in 1958 when prof. Bolesław Krupiński - a prominent Polish scientist and mining engineer organized the first congress in Warsaw. Prof. Krupiński was the WMC Chairman until 1972.
The WMC is the UN - afiliated organization. It is run by the permanent secretariat seated in Poland.

AIMS AND OBJECTIVES

The aims and objectives for which the Society
is established are as under:

(a) To represent India at the World Mining Congress ("WMC"), an organization affiliated to the United Nations, as the "National Committee" of India.

(b) To promote the objectives for which the WMC is established i.e. to promote and foster the development of the mining and mineral sector with emphasis on the Indian mining sector, by putting the natural resources of the country to their optimal use, for the overall economic improvement of the country by considering:

The viability of the potential resources, particularly the natural resources and all the means of production, transportation, transformation and utilization of such resources with a view to ensure sustainable development so as to achieve a balance between consumption and preservation of mineral resources and economic growth of the nation; and

The social and environmental aspects in the utilization of all mineral resources.

(c) To collect, print and publish information, statistics and data on mmmg sector, including data pertaining to the production, consumption and utilization of all minerals.

(d) To collaborate or enter into arrangements with other national and/or international organizations/ institutions in the mining/ mineral sector for conducting joint projects in the field of mining/ minerals and to send and/or receive delegations to/ from such national and/or international organizations/ institutions for the purpose of promoting and supporting scientific and technical co-operation for national and international progress in the field of solid mineral development and development of natural mineral resources.

(e) To foster contacts and consultations through commissioned research, workshops, seminars, and conferences among academia, policy framers, managers, entrepreneurs and others interested in furthering socio-economic progress through international cooperation.

(f) To organize and participate in technical and scientific studies, hold workshops, seminars, conferences, fairs, lectures, educational training and awareness programmes related to mining sciences, advancement of technology in the field of mining, economics and health and safety aspects of mining operations, environmental protection and matters incidental thereto and to compile, collate, edit and publish technical reports and papers related to the aims and objects of the Society.

(g) To foster and ensure collection and dissemination of inforrnati national and at the international level, including exchange and of such information among its members, with respect to the development of mining science, technology, economics and healthy and safety aspects of mining operations, environmental  protection and matters incidental thereto.

(h) To nominate members to the bodies of the WMC provisions of the constitution of the Society.

(i) To organize, finance and manage activities of the WMC when held in India.

(j) To set up committees or sub-committees, as may be required for catering to the specific requirements of mining/ mineral sector or any segment of this sector.

(k) To study the problems faced by the mmmg industry in general and to conduct necessary research with a view to evolve solutions to the problems.

(l) To undertake basic scientific research as may be necessary in the field of mining from the view point of future advances in mining technology in consonance with national priorities.

(m) To foster networking among the members of the Society on Issues concerning the mining industry.

(n) To promote and conduct scientific research on socio-economic and politico[1]economic issues of concern to the mining industry and to improve linkages between researchers, professional managers, policy makers and negotiators.

(o) To take appropriate steps to formulate policies for development of the national mineral resources of India.

(p) To take all steps by lawful means, which may be necessary for promoting, supporting and making representation in respect of any approved legislation or other actions affecting the interest of the Society.
